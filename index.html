02 / Reading the Plans
Project Understanding

We've read your drawings.

We've gone through the Gelfand Residence plans for the Hideout lot — 4,876 sq ft total across a 2,409 sq ft main level over a 2,467 sq ft walkout/daylight basement, with a 995 sq ft three-car garage rotated roughly 110° off the main footprint. The framing scope is driven by three things on these sheets: the exposed glulam beams and glulam truss in the great room/dining/kitchen, the vaulted ceiling work at 12'-1 1/8" over a 10'-1 1/8" plate, and a roof with 11-plus ridges mixing 6:12, 4:12, and 2:12 pitches with 3-ft overhangs.

All walls are 2x6 exterior / 2x4 interior stick frame per the plans, which is our bread and butter. We've priced this as a labor-only scope — you provide lumber and hardware, we provide the crew, fasteners, forklift and crane mobilization, and daily cleanup. Structural sheets S2.1/S2.2 weren't in the set we received; if a steel beam schedule shows up there, we'll price that separately rather than guess at it now.

05 / Intelligence
Three Things You Should Know

Site-specific observations from reading the plans.

★ Important

Angled garage wing will drive the roof framing schedule

The garage rotates roughly 110° off the main house, which collides with 11+ ridges at varying elevations and a stack of valleys where the wings meet. We'll want to set the main ridge first and work the garage tie-in last so we're cutting valley jacks to real field measurements, not plan dimensions. Plan on roof framing taking about a third of the 12-week duration.

✕ Watch

Exposed glulams in the great room need a crane day, not a forklift day

The RCP shows exposed glulam beams plus a glulam truss over the great room/dining/kitchen at the 12'-1" vaulted ceiling. Because these are architectural-grade and stay visible, we handle them with soft slings and stage the crane pick separately from the rest of the main-level deck. Budget a half-day premium and confirm beam delivery is sequenced before sheathing.

✕ Watch

Walkout basement tie-in depends on final grade certification

Bedrooms 1-3 open to daylight on the downhill side, which means the lower-level wall heights and window/door head heights are driven by surveyed grade, not the plan. We'd like to walk the pad with the excavator before we cut lower-level plates — catching a 6" grade variance now is a no-cost fix, catching it after sheathing is a change order.

◆ Note

Structural sheets S2.1/S2.2 not in the set we reviewed

Our number assumes no structural steel based on what's visible, but the S-series wasn't included in this excerpt. If the engineer specs steel flitch plates or moment frames at the great room glulam truss, that's added scope. Send those sheets when available and we'll confirm or revise within 48 hours — no surprises at framing start.

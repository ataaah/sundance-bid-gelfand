Local Intelligence

Built for Hideout conditions, not generic Utah conditions

This residence sits in a mountain-build environment — Hideout — where framing is affected by snow-load design, wind exposure, cold-weather sequencing, access, staging, inspections, and limited seasonal construction windows. We priced this frame as a custom mountain project, not as a valley production frame.

Snow-load awareness

Mountain-area homes should be reviewed using site-specific Utah snow-load tools and engineered roof design — especially where elevation and roof geometry create drift and load concentration risk. The structural engineer owns the snow-load design; we frame to it.

Mountain schedule realism

Roof framing, glulam setting, and dry-in sequencing matter more in this climate — weather delays can turn open framing into a schedule AND protection problem. We build a realistic window into the schedule, not an optimistic one.

Site logistics

Crane access, forklift routes, lumber staging, waste bins, and material delivery timing get confirmed before crew mobilization. On a mountain lot, production is not only crew speed — it's material flow.

Plan control on site

Complete stamped/acknowledged plans must be available on site during framing. We track plan revisions and confirm the set we're framing from matches the set the inspector will reference.

Hideout jobsite hours

Per Hideout town code, construction (including grading) is limited to 7:00 AM – 7:00 PM Monday through Friday and 10:00 AM – 5:00 PM on Saturday, Sunday, and federal holidays. Staging stays within the approved limits of disturbance.

Framing Attention Zones

Five regions of the build that need extra care.

Schematic Frame · Indicative Only Drag to rotate
A

Walkout-to-Main Floor Interface

The lower-level walkout rim and uphill mudsill have to land at consistent elevations so the main-level floor system, exterior door ROs, and deck ledgers all align on a sloped site.

Our approach. Survey-verified rim elevations before plates are cut, single crew owning mudsill through main-level deck so transitions don't get handed off.

B

Rotated Garage Tie-In

The ~110° garage rotation creates non-square wall plate geometry and a custom roof intersection where the garage roof meets the main house — high risk for valley framing errors.

Our approach. Independent layout off a control line, dry-fit the connector framing, cut intersecting valleys from a field-built story pole rather than relying on tables.

C

Great Room Vaulted Volume + Glulam Set

12'-1" ceiling with exposed glulam beams and a glulam truss — the framing and the architectural structure happen in the same volume and have to be sequenced together.

Our approach. Stage scaffold once, set glulams with crane and soft slings before sloped ceiling joists, protect exposed faces through close-in.

D

Multi-Ridge Roof System

11+ ridges at varying elevations with mixed 2:12 / 4:12 / 6:12 pitches and 3-ft overhangs — every valley is a custom cut and fascia plane control matters on a view home.

Our approach. Pre-built cut list from the framing plans, jigged outlookers for consistent overhang, string-lined fascia at every pitch break.

E

View-Side Window and Door Wall

Downhill elevation carries the larger glazing and the home's signature view — any RO drift or out-of-plane wall shows in trim, cladding, and from inside the great room.

Our approach. Headers staged separately, ROs verified against window schedule when issued, wall plane straightened before sheathing on the view elevation.

Custom Build Sequence

Eight phases. Each one specific to this house.

Schematic Frame · Not Construction Documents Phase 01 · Pre-frame Drag to rotate · scroll to advance phases
01

Pre-frame coordination

Stage materials and prep the sloped Hideout site for a 4,876 sqft walkout build with an angled 3-stall garage wing.

Crew 3 · forklift · material trailer · tarps · transit level · chop saw station
Phase details
  • Verify foundation elevations on the daylight/downhill side where Bedrooms 1, 2, and 3 open to grade — walkout step-downs need to be dead-on before any plates go down
  • Stage 2x6 exterior and 2x4 interior stick packs near the main footprint, with a separate drop for the rotated ~110° garage wing so we're not double-handling lumber
  • Pre-sort and protect the exposed glulam beams and the great room glulam truss under cover — these are finish-grade and we won't band-saw them in the mud
  • Confirm S2.1/S2.2 structural sheets are in hand before we start; the architectural set doesn't show a steel schedule and we need that cleared
  • Walk the 11+ ridge roof layout with the lead carpenter so everyone sees the complexity-4 roof before day one
Week 0.0 → +0.4w
02

Layout & foundation verification

Snap plates for the 2,467 sqft walkout lower and chalk the irregular footprint including the angled garage wing.

Crew 3 · chalk lines · laser level · plate stock · powder-actuated nailer · transit
Phase details
  • Chalk lower-level exterior 2x6 walls first along the daylight wall so the bedroom door/window rough openings line up with the patio and hot tub locations
  • Lay out the ~110° garage rotation off the main level control lines — we set that angle once and reference it for every plate above
  • Mark bearing points on the lower-level deck for the main-level glulam posts now, before any joists go down
  • Plate the 2x4 interior partitions after exterior is chalked, paying attention to the footprint-complexity-5 jogs
  • Double-check ceiling height transitions — 10'-1 1/8" typical with 12'-1 1/8" raised great room
Week 0.4 → +0.6w
03

Lower level / floor system

Frame the 2,467 sqft walkout lower level, working from the daylight wall uphill into the unexcavated side.

Crew 5 · air nailers · forklift · wall jacks · scaffolding · circular saws
Phase details
  • Stand the daylight wall first — Bedrooms 1, 2, and 3 with their grade-level door and window openings — so we can brace off finished grade
  • Work clockwise from the walkout face into the buried foundation walls, sheathing as we go to keep the long downhill wall plumb
  • Set 2x6 exterior / 2x4 interior throughout; lower level is mostly flat-ceiling so joist layout is straightforward once bearing is set
  • Block and strap all main-level glulam post bearing locations down through the lower-level walls to the foundation
  • Run the lower-level deck (main floor system) before moving up — solid platform makes the glulam set safer
Week 1.0 → +1.9w
04

Main level framing

Stick-frame the 2,409 sqft main level with 10'-1" walls and prepped pockets for the great room glulam truss and beams.

Crew 5 · wall jacks · air nailers · forklift · scaffolding · 10' step ladders
Phase details
  • Stand main-level 2x6 exterior walls at 10'-1 1/8" — taller plates means wall jacks and a 4-man stand on the long runs
  • Frame the great room walls to 12'-1 1/8" with proper headers and bearing for the glulam truss that lands there
  • Hold off sheathing on the great room gable ends until the glulams are flown — we need the swing room
  • Frame the angled garage-wing connection walls last on this phase so we can dial the ~110° geometry against the main box
  • Pre-cut and label vaulted ceiling sloped top plates for the two vaulted areas before walls go up
Week 2.9 → +2.6w
05

Garage wing framing

Frame the 995 sqft 3-stall garage wing rotated ~110° off the main house axis.

Crew 4 · wall jacks · forklift · air nailers · header beams · framing squares
Phase details
  • Build the garage after the main ridge is established so we can reference the rotation off a finished structure, not chalk lines
  • Stand the three stall openings with proper header sizing — long garage door headers carry significant load on this complex roof
  • Tie the garage wing back to the main house at the angled intersection with engineered connections; this is the trickiest geometry on the job
  • Frame the garage ceiling system ready to receive its portion of the roof — multiple ridges land on this wing
  • Sheathe walls before roof framing starts so the rotated wing is rigid
Week 5.5 → +1.2w
06

Glulam / beam coordination

Fly and set the exposed glulam beams and the great room glulam truss that define the main-level vaulted ceilings.

Crew 5 · crane · rigging straps · moving blankets · come-alongs · impact drivers · scaffolding
Phase details
  • Crane in the great room glulam truss first — it's the biggest piece and sets the ridge reference for the surrounding vaulted framing
  • Set exposed glulam beams across the great room / dining / kitchen per the RCP, protecting finish faces with blankets through the rigging
  • Verify bearing pockets and hardware (saddles, knife plates from the S-sheets) are correct before releasing the crane on each pick
  • These are finish-exposed members — every fastener placement matters; no spare nail holes
  • Coordinate with the roof phase so common rafters and ridge beams can land on the glulams the same week
Week 6.7 → +1.0w
07

Roof framing

Cut and stack the complexity-4 roof with 11+ ridges, hips, valleys, and 3-ft overhangs over the main house and rotated garage.

Crew 5 · crane · forklift · roof jacks · harnesses · air nailers · circular saws
Phase details
  • Start with the dominant 6:12 main ridge over the great room glulam truss, then work outward into the hips and valleys
  • Stick-frame the two vaulted ceiling areas with sloped rafters tied to the exposed glulams — no trusses possible where glulams show
  • Frame the 2:12 and 4:12 secondary roof areas after the main 6:12 is sheathed so we have a working platform
  • Hold the 3-ft overhangs consistent on all eaves — outlookers and sub-fascia get pre-cut in batches to keep the line clean
  • Tie the rotated garage roof into the main house valleys last; this intersection is where the 11+ ridge count gets earned
  • Sheathe progressively to keep the structure stiff as we work through the multi-elevation ridges
Week 7.7 → +2.4w
08

Sheathing & framing punchlist

Walk the 4,876 sqft structure, tighten everything, and hand off a framed shell ready for mechanicals and exterior trades.

Crew 3 · impact drivers · punch tools · ladders · broom and magnet sweeper · tape measures
Phase details
  • Walk every vaulted ceiling and the great room glulam truss with the GC — backing, blocking, and exposed-member protection all verified
  • Re-check the angled garage-to-main intersection for plumb and connection hardware now that loads are fully on
  • Install all remaining blocking for cabinets, tile backers, stair stringers, and railing posts at the walkout patio and decks
  • Confirm every rough opening on the daylight wall matches door/window schedule — those are the visible elevation
  • Clean up cut piles, pull stray nails, and turn over a broom-clean deck on both levels
Week 10.1 → +1.9w
